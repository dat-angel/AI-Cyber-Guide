# AI-Cyber-Guide

Hello, World!

This repository houses **The AI Cyber Guide**, a practical toolkit for AI-assisted incident communications. It started as our 2024 lab session at RSAC and now includes the updated **WiCyS 2026 incident comms framework**.

- **Current version:** v0.2 — *WiCyS 2026 Framework Update*

---

## What is The AI Cyber Guide?

The AI Cyber Guide leverages the power of AI to streamline incident response and enhance communication, ultimately saving time and building trust during critical situations.

It focuses on:
- Using AI to help you understand an incident and its regulatory impact.
- Generating clear, audience-specific messages during high-stress events.
- Practicing these skills *before* you’re in the middle of a live incident.

---

## What’s New – WiCyS 2026

If you’re coming from **WiCyS 2026 – “Human Communication at Machine Speed”**, start here.

The **WiCyS 2026 update** adds a fast-start framework for incident communications across three core audiences, plus a 7‑day practice challenge.

### Three Audiences, Three Needs

| Audience      | Need              | Message Focus                     |
|--------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Engineering  | Immediate action  | What to do **now**               |
| Executive    | Business impact   | Risk, scope, and timeline        |
| Customer     | Transparent guide | What happened + what we’re doing |

### Four Pillars (Every Message)

| Pillar      | Question | Answer                     |
|------------|----------|----------------------------|
| **Target** | Who?     | Eng / Exec / Customer      |
| **Message**| What?    | Impact + Action + Update   |
| **Channel**| Where?   | Slack / Email / Status page|
| **Timing** | When?    | Immediate / Hourly / Daily |

Every message should clearly state:
- Impact
- Action required (if any)
- Next update time

📄 **Fast-start framework:** see `2026-wicys/WiCyS-2026-Handout.md`.

## Your 7‑Day Practice Challenge

Use this repo as a focused, week-long exercise:

- [ ] **Download the repo** and skim the WiCyS handout.
- [ ] **Customize the framework** for your org’s audiences and channels.
- [ ] **Test the “/security-brief” style prompts** in your AI tool of choice.
- [ ] **Assign audience owners** (Eng / Exec / Customer comms).
- [ ] **Run a tabletop** using the RSAC scenario as your incident.
- [ ] **Share the framework** with your team and capture feedback.

## Background – RSAC 2024 Lab

This project started as **LAB2-T01: “Set Yourself Up for Success: Build Trust during Incidents by Leveraging AI”** at RSAC 2024, presented by [Renana Friedlich](https://www.linkedin.com/in/renana-friedlich-barsky-aa523049/) and [Beatriz Datangel](https://www.linkedin.com/in/bzdata).

The original lab materials live in `2024-rsac-lab/` and focus on:
- Exploring differences between AI tools.
- Assuming an identity and incident scenario.
- Assessing whether an incident is reportable in certain jurisdictions.
- Generating messages for specific audiences about this incident.

Key files:
- `2024-rsac-lab/RSAC Scenario.md` – OmniRetail scenario background.
- `Comms.md` and related templates – example messages for executives, customers, and employees.
